2. Background of QSSA

  • The emergence of QSSA was driven by the need to simplify and understand complex reaction mechanisms in chemical kinetics.
  • When the concentration of intermediates changes rapidly over time, accounting for this in reaction rate equations can lead to complexity. In such cases, the Quasi-Steady-State Approximation(QSSA) is used to assume that the concentration of intermediates remains constant, simplifying the reaction rate equations.


3. What is QSSA(Quasi-Steady-State Approximation)?

  • 화학 및 생물학에서 반응 메커니즘을 모델링할 때 사용되는 일반적인 기법 중 하나
  • 반응 속도가 빠른 중간체의 생성 또는 소멸이 느린 경우에 유효
  • 반응 속도가 빠른 중간체의 농도가 빠르게 변화하지 않고 일정한 것으로 가정하는데, 이는 그러한 중간체의 생성 및 소멸이 빠르기 때문에 상태가 균형에 도달하고 속도가 매우 빨라서 상대적으로 안정적인 것으로 간주
  • 이러한 중간체의 농도는 시간에 따라 거의 변하지 않는 것으로 가정


Application case with QSSA

  • Fast Transient States : When intermediates are rapidly formed and consumed in the initial stages of a chemical reaction
  • Concurrent Reactions: QSSA is useful when multiple reaction pathways compete, and a specific intermediate is rapidly formed and consumed via different pathways
  • Fast Equilibrium Reactions: QSSA can be applied to reactions where equilibrium is rapidly established
  • Stepwise Reactions: QSSA can be applied to reactions that proceed through multiple steps.
  • Enzyme kinetics Model with QSSA
    • Michaelis–Menten kinetics for the basic enzyme reaction
    • Hill kinetics for cooperative enzymatic models for enzyme inhibition processes
    • Goldbetter–Koshland function in models of futile cycles
